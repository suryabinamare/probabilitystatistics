t-Distribution:

Learning Objectives:

  • Compute the p-values for given t-scores and degrees of freedom.
  • Find the t-score corresponding to a given p-value and degrees of freedom when
    • when $p$ is the area to the left of the t-score.
    • when $p$ is the area to the right of the t-score.

Introduction:

The t-distribution is like z-distribution, with the density curve that is bell-shaped symmetric with respect to vertical line through 0. The distribution has mean of 0 and stanard deviation of 1. Both tails of the curve extend to infinity. But the tails of t-distribution are thicker than that of z-distribution, which reflects increased uncertainty.

The area under the density curve is 1. P-value represents the area of a region under the curve which is shaded blue. t-score is plotted along the horizontal axis as the red dot. Here,

  • t-score: This is the value of the t-statistic you want to evaluate. This value is plotted along the horizontal axis as the red dot. You can find the t-score when you know the P-value and the degrees of freedom (df).
  • P-value: This value represents the area under the curve to the right of the t-score and is shaded blue in the plot. For \(t_0\), the p-value is given by \(p(t \leq t_0)\).
    If you want to find the area to the right of \(t_0\),
    \(p(t\geq t_0) = 1 - p(t\leq t_0)\)
