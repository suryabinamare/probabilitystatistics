Introduction:

The F-distribution is used primarily in analysis of variance (ANOVA) and regression analysis to compare variances and test hypotheses about the equality of means across multiple groups. It is characterized by two parameters: the degrees of freedom for the numerator (dfn) and the degrees of freedom for the denominator (dfd). The F-distribution is right-skewed and takes only non-negative values.

  1. F-score: The value of the F-statistic you want to evaluate. This value is plotted along the horizontal axis as the red dot.
  2. P-value: The probability of observing a value as extreme as the F-score. This value represents the area under the curve to the right of the F-score and is shaded blue in the plot.

Shape of the F-Distribution

The shape of the F-distribution depends on the degrees of freedom for the numerator (dfn) and the denominator (dfd). As the degrees of freedom increase, the distribution becomes more symmetric and approaches a normal distribution. Below you can see the F-distribution for different degrees of freedom.
